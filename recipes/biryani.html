Biryani

Description

To make chicken biryani, begin by marinating 1 kg bone-in chicken with 1 cup yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, biryani masala, lemon juice, salt, and a little oil for at least one hour; meanwhile soak 2 cups basmati rice for 30 minutes, then boil it in plenty of water with salt and whole spices (bay leaf, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon) until the rice is about 70% cooked and drain it. In a heavy pot, heat ghee or oil and fry thinly sliced onions until golden, add green chilies and ginger-garlic paste, then tomatoes and cook until soft and aromatic; add the marinated chicken and cook until it is partially done and the masala thickens. Lower the heat and layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken, topping it with chopped mint, coriander, fried onions, and saffron milk (or rose water), then cover tightly and cook on very low heat (dum) for about 20-25 minutes until the chicken is tender and the rice is fully cooked, rest for a few minutes, gently fluff, and serve hot with raita and lemon wedges.

Ingredients

    For Rice

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 6-8 cups water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4 green cardamoms
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp salt

    • For Chicken Marinade

  • 1 kg chicken (bone-in preferred)
  • 1 cup thick yogurt
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1½ tsp red chili powder
  • 1½ tsp biryani masala (or garam masala)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil

    • For Biryani Masala Base

  • 3 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 cup chopped tomatoes
  • ½ cup chopped mint leaves
  • ½ cup chopped coriander leaves

    • For Layering

  • ¼ cup warm milk + a pinch of saffron (or ½ tsp rose water)
  • Extra fried onions (optional)

Steps

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes.
  2. Marinate chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, spices, lemon juice, salt, and oil; rest for at least 1 hour.
  3. Boil water with salt and whole spices, cook the rice until 70% done, then drain and set aside.
  4. Heat ghee or oil in a heavy pot and fry sliced onions until golden brown.
  5. Add green chilies and ginger-garlic paste; sauté until fragrant.
  6. Add tomatoes and cook until soft and oil separates.
  7. Add the marinated chicken and cook until partially cooked and the masala thickens.
  8. Reduce heat and spread half the cooked rice over the chicken.
  9. Sprinkle mint, coriander, fried onions, and saffron milk.
  10. Add the remaining rice and repeat the toppings.
  11. Cover the pot tightly and cook on very low heat (dum) for 20-25 minutes.
  12. Turn off heat, let it rest for 5-10 minutes, gently mix, and serve hot with raita.
